## How to Switch Roles

### Desktop (Large Screens)
1. Look at the **left sidebar**
2. Find the **"Active Role"** section near the top
3. See your current role displayed with an icon
4. Click the **colored icon button** on the right
5. Select your desired role from the modal

### Mobile & Tablet (Small Screens)
**Method 1 - Via Burger Menu**:
1. Tap the **menu icon** (â˜°) in the top-left
2. Burger menu slides in from left
3. See your user info and current role badge
4. Tap **"Switch Role"** button
5. Select your desired role from the modal

**Method 2 - From Settings** (Alternative):
1. Navigate to Settings
2. Find "Role Management" section
3. Tap "Switch Role"

## Role Switching Behavior

### What Happens When You Switch
1. **Instant Navigation**: Automatically redirects to the new role's dashboard

2. **Visual Changes**:

3. **Data Separation**: Each role maintains separate data
   - Patient medications are separate from caregiver's dependents

   - Next login returns to your last used role

## Registration & First-Time Setup

### Choosing Your Initial Role

During registration, you'll see three large cards:

**Patient Card** (Blue):
- "For managing my own medications"
- Includes fields for Date of Birth and Gender
- These help generate a personalized avatar

**Caregiver Card** (Orange):
- "I care for family or friends"
- No DOB/gender needed initially

**Healthcare Professional Card** (Purple):
- "I'm a doctor or healthcare provider"
- Professional interface with patient management

**Note**: You can switch roles anytime after registration, so don't worry about choosing "wrong"!

## Use Cases

### Example 1: Patient Who Also Caregivers
Sarah is 45 years old. She:
- Takes her own medications (diabetes, blood pressure)
- Also cares for her elderly mother

**Solution**: Sarah registers as "Patient" first
1. Sets up her own medications in Patient role
2. Switches to Caregiver role
3. Adds her mother as a dependent
4. Manages both seamlessly by switching roles

### Example 2: Doctor with Personal Medications
Dr. Ahmed is a cardiologist who:
- Prescribes medications to patients
- Also takes his own medications for asthma

**Solution**: Dr. Ahmed registers as "Healthcare Professional"
1. Manages his patients in Doctor role
2. Switches to Patient role for personal medications
3. Keeps professional and personal completely separate

### Example 3: Family Caregiver for Multiple People
Maria cares for:
- Her father (post-stroke)
- Her husband (heart condition)
- Her daughter (anxiety medication)

**Solution**: Maria registers as "Caregiver"
1. Adds all three as dependents
2. Manages their medication schedules
3. Views combined analytics to track adherence

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I have multiple roles at once?**
A: Yes! One account can use all three roles. Just switch between them as needed.

**Q: Do I need to set up each role separately?**
A: Yes. Each role has its own data:
- Patient: Your medications
- Caregiver: Your dependents' information
- Doctor: Your patients' data

**Q: Will my medications disappear when I switch roles?**
A: No. Your Patient medications are always saved. Switching roles just changes which dashboard you see.

**Q: Can I switch roles mid-task?**
A: Yes, but unsaved changes will be lost. Save your work before switching.

**Q: Which role should I register as?**
A: Choose based on your primary use:
- Taking your own meds? â†’ Patient
- Caring for someone else? â†’ Caregiver (but you can still add yourself later)
- Healthcare provider? â†’ Doctor

**Q: How do I know which role I'm currently using?**
A: Look for:
- Color of the interface (blue/orange/purple)
- "Active Role" text in sidebar (desktop)
- Role badge in burger menu (mobile)
- Page title in top bar

## Privacy & Security

### Data Separation
- Each role's data is isolated
- Patient can't see Caregiver's dependents
- Caregiver can't access Patient's personal meds
- Doctor's patient list is completely separate

### Shared Access (Coming Soon)
- Caregivers can request access to Patient's medications

## Best Practices

1. **Start Simple**: Begin with one role, add others as needed

3. **Check Your Role**: Before adding data, confirm you're in the right role
4. **Use Color Cues**: Train yourself to recognize blue/orange/purple
5. **Save Before Switching**: Complete your current task first
